Web Design Principles

    3 additional web design principles are:

    Fritt's Law

    Pulls the users' eye in to see what's most important.

    Hick's Law

    effectively means you want to remove clutter and show your users only the most essential options they need. For example, most websites will give a clear choice between “Save” or “Cancel” and “Yes” or “No” when confirming some action.

    Law of Common Region

    Simply states that if elements on a page are grouped together closely, they are perceived as connected to one another.
